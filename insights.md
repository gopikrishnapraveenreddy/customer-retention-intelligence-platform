# Customer Churn Risk Analysis - Insights

## Summary

This project analyzed **7,032 valid telecom customer records** to understand customer churn behavior. The analysis shows that **1,869 customers churned**, giving an overall churn rate of **26.58%**.

---

## Main Insights

### 1. Overall Churn Rate

The overall churn rate is approximately **26.58%**, meaning around one out of every four customers left the company.

This level of churn is significant because customer retention is usually less expensive than acquiring new customers.

---

### 2. Contract Type Pattern

Customers on **Month-to-month contracts** show the highest churn rate at **42.71%**.

This suggests that customers without long-term commitments are more likely to leave. Annual or two-year contracts appear to be associated with stronger retention.

---

### 3. Internet Service Pattern

Customers using **Fiber optic internet service** show the highest churn rate at **41.89%**.

This may indicate pricing concerns, service quality issues, or stronger competition for that service segment.

---

### 4. Payment Method Pattern

Customers using **Electronic check** show the highest churn rate at **45.29%**.

This pattern suggests that payment behavior may be associated with churn risk. Customers using automatic payment methods may be more stable compared with customers using manual payment methods.

---

### 5. Tenure Pattern

Customers in the **0-12 Months tenure group** show the highest churn rate at **47.68%**.

This indicates that newer customers are more likely to churn, making early customer engagement and onboarding important.

---

### 6. Monthly Charges Pattern

Churned customers have a higher average monthly charge of **$74.44**, compared with **$61.31** for retained customers.

This suggests that customers paying higher monthly bills may be more price-sensitive or may expect better service value.

---

## Business Recommendations

- Focus retention campaigns on month-to-month customers.
- Offer incentives for customers to move from monthly contracts to annual contracts.
- Improve onboarding programs for customers in their first 12 months.
- Review the customer experience for fiber optic users.
- Provide targeted discounts or service bundles for high-charge customers.
- Encourage automatic payment methods to improve customer stability.

---

## Limitation

This analysis is based on historical customer data and identifies patterns associated with churn. It does not prove that any single factor directly causes churn.

Further analysis using predictive modeling would be needed to estimate individual customer churn risk.
